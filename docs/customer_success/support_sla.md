# Support & SLA Guidelines (Draft)

## Support Tiers
- **Community (default)**: GitHub issues, best-effort response.
- **Design Partner**: Email/Slack channel, response within 1 business day, access to weekly office hours.
- **Enterprise (future)**: 24/5 support, response within 4 hours for Sev-1, dedicated solutions engineer.

## Severity Levels & Targets
| Severity | Examples | Response | Resolution Target |
|----------|----------|----------|-------------------|
| Sev-1 | Production outage, data corruption, security incident | 4 hours | 24 hours workaround, 5 days fix |
| Sev-2 | Major feature malfunction, blocked experimentation | 1 business day | 7 days |
| Sev-3 | Minor bug, documentation gap | 2 business days | Best effort |

## Escalation Workflow
1. Log issue in GitHub (or support desk for enterprise).
2. Triage by Product/Engineering lead.
3. Assign owner and severity, update status board.
4. Communicate progress in weekly report or sooner for Sev-1/2.

## Communication Channels
- GitHub Issues: bug reports, feature requests, community questions.
- Slack/Teams (design partners): quick feedback and triage updates.
- Email (enterprise): escalation and SLA tracking.

## Reporting
- Maintain changelog of resolved issues and release notes.
- Share monthly summary covering ticket volume, response times, and planned updates.
